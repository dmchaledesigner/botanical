TAKE A LOOK AROUND

Maylands is bordered on three sides by the beautiful Swan River foreshore with its lakes, parklands and cycle paths, a yacht club and a golf club. The thriving precincts of Eighth Avenue and Whatley Crescent are buzzing with eclectic new shops, small bars, cafés and restaurants.

You’re beautifully connected within a 1km radius - from the river, to Maylands Park Shopping Centre (including IGA and Coles), golf course and yacht club.
The northern end of Kathleen Avenue connects with Eighth Avenue, where you’ll find an eclectic mix of shops and trendy cafes, as well as Maylands’ $21 million multipurpose centre, The RISE. Meanwhile, Maylands Peninsula Primary School, KIDS Open Learning School, St Columba’s Primary School and Chisholm Catholic College and Mercy Hospital are all within a ten-minute drive.

A day at Kathleen Avenue Maylands is easy to fill. Take an early morning stroll along the beautiful Swan River as the morning unfolds, before hitting the strip for brunch with friends at one of the many chic eateries including Mrs S. and Milk’d.

The afternoon is yours to enjoy, with a raft of attractions within easy walking distance. Head to Maylands Peninsula Golf Course for a round, kayak up the river, or work out at The RISE.
As the evening draws near, enjoy a quick meal at Rifo’s Café before taking a train into the Perth CBD, where an array of entertainment will lead you through the night. Or you may choose to stay local with a nightcap at Swallow bar. Chic, contemporary living this close to the water’s edge won’t last!

The Design

The unique architectural design of the boutique apartments at Botanical comprises individual groups vertically articulated in a ‘townhouse’ style with individual roofs of varying elements.

Vertical brick features combined with rendered walls and wood detailing make a unique statement on the street elevation. A skillion roof to the second floor units further enhances the street appearance.

The internal design has been created for residents to enjoy enhanced air circulation and a modern style of living, with full width balconies taking advantage of the West Australian climate.

The resultant architectural statement is a building reflecting the elements synonymous with the architectural icon Frank Lloyd Wright.

Joe Chindarsi has over 14 years of industry experience, both in the commercial, educational, residential and public sectors.
